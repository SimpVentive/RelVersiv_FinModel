How the Model Works

This stress tester is built directly from the RelVersiv_Model_v3.xlsx Excel file. Every number you see starts from the same base assumptions as the Excel model — sliders let you deviate from those assumptions to explore upside and downside scenarios.


Three layers:


1. Revenue Engine — 7 revenue streams, each driven by entity counts (hospitals, physio clinics, corporates) that ramp each year. Adjust entity counts via the Y5 sliders; the model applies the Excel growth curve proportionally.


2. Cost Structure — COGS (30% of revenue), Acquisition cost (20%), fixed staff salaries (7 components, editable), variable physio (Y1–Y2 only, scales with physio count), and depreciation on VR headsets and POD units.


3. Valuation — DCF using WACC and terminal growth rate, plus a Revenue Multiple exit method. The Blended EV averages both. Post-money = Exit Value ÷ VC Return Multiple.

Revenue Stream Formulas
1. Hospital Therapy
Hosp_entities × Episodes/entity × Sessions × Price
2. Physio Therapy
Physio_entities × Episodes/entity × Sessions × Price
3. Corporate Prognosis
New_orgs × Emp × Pen × Price

+ Prior_orgs × 12% newhires × Emp × Pen × Price
4. Hospital POD Sessions
Hosp_entities × Episodes × 20% conv × Sessions × ₹250
5. Corporate POD Sessions
Corp × Emp × Pen × 20% POD_conv × Sessions × ₹250
6. Home Rental
Hosp_entities × Episodes × 30% × 2 months × ₹5,000
7. POD Device Rental (PaaS)
(Hosp + Physio) × 60% adoption × ₹20,000/mo × 12
P&L Waterfall Explained
Revenue — Sum of all 7 streams above.

COGS (30%) — Cost of Revenue: covers VR headset wear, cloud hosting, content licensing, and remote support. Applied as 30% of total revenue per the Excel assumptions.

Gross Profit = Revenue − COGS. This should be 70% at base. If COGS slider is moved up, margins compress.

Acquisition Cost (20%) — Sales, marketing, and channel partner costs. Applied as 20% of total revenue.

Staff Salaries — 7 fixed components (editable in the salary panel), plus variable physio in Y1–Y2. Fixed staff grows 10%/year from Y3 onwards. Variable physio = physio centres ÷ 12 × 3 physios × ₹3.6L/year.

EBITDA = Gross Profit − Acquisition − Salaries. This is the operating profitability before asset depreciation.

VR Headset Depreciation — 40% declining balance on headset asset base. One headset per new entity added each year.

POD Depreciation — 15% declining balance on POD asset base. PODs deployed = entities × 60% adoption.

EBIT = EBITDA − Depreciation. Also shown as EBIT Margin %.

Tax — 25% on positive EBIT only. Loss years carry no tax.

PAT = EBIT − Tax. Net profit after all charges.

Free Cash Flow = PAT + Depreciation − VR Capex − POD Capex. This is the cash the business actually generates for investors.
Valuation Methodology
DCF (Discounted Cash Flow)
Each year's Free Cash Flow is discounted back to present value using WACC. The sum of discounted FCFs = PV of operating cash flows.

PV of FCF = Σ [ FCF_yr ÷ (1+WACC)^yr ]
Terminal Value — Captures value beyond Y5 using the Gordon Growth Model. Terminal FCF = Y5 FCF × (1 + g) ÷ (WACC − g), then discounted back 5 years. Watch this carefully — it typically represents 60–80% of total DCF value.
TV = Y5_FCF × (1+g) ÷ (WACC − g) ÷ (1+WACC)^5
Revenue Multiple Exit — Simpler method: Y5 Revenue × Exit Multiple (10x), discounted at WACC. More commonly used by VCs to cross-check DCF.

PV Exit = Y5_Rev × 10x ÷ (1+WACC)^5
Blended EV = (DCF EV + PV Exit) ÷ 2. This is the headline valuation.

Post-Money Valuation — Excel uses: Exit Value ÷ VC Return Multiple = ₹499.6Cr ÷ 12x = ₹41.63Cr. This is what you quote to investors as the current implied valuation.
Post-Money = (Y5 Rev × Exit Multiple) ÷ VC Return = ₹41.63Cr
